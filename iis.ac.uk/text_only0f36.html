Field Trips



The aim of the field trips is to provide a holistic understanding of the historical content that students will eventually bring to life in the classroom. STEP students undertake two field trips in the first year of the programme, one to Andalusia, Spain, and the other to Cairo, Egypt (subject to FCO approval). The trips are focused on visits to Muslim architecture of Andalusia and Fatimid-era Cairo, but also provide the opportunity to visit art and architecture from other eras. 

The educational field trips allow students to witness the contemporary use of the architectural sites and to learn about the structures in relation to both secular and religious functions, as well as gain an appreciation of their aesthetics. The trips help students to teach the IIS’ Secondary Curriculum, in which they learn about historical and contemporary Muslim civilisations including about such urban centres as Cordoba (Spain) and Cairo (Egypt).

 

Field Trip Experiences:

“I experienced “Fatimid Cairo” not just by visiting the Fatimid mosques and monuments but its influence resonated in many other structures built by later rulers. It was nostalgic walking through Shari' al-Mu'izz and going inside the mosque of Al-Hakim and Al-Azhar mosque. Once inside these mosques, we sat on the floor and marvelled at the vast courtyards, the continuous arches and the intricately decorated domes and mihrabs. I find Cairo to be a masterpiece of time – its canvas displays remarkable feats of humanity.”

Muniza Ahmed, Cohort 2

 

“STEP has been an invaluable experience. It has gifted me with a family of students who are working together to reach a common goal – becoming successful educators. Our field trips and teaching experiences have been the best part, which has brought a practical approach to the curriculum that we teach.”

Shehzeen Sunderji, Cohort 5 


 “The programme has exposed me not only to the history of Islamic thought and expression through a rich 1400 years of history but has also allowed me to situate the Ismaili community, my community, within that history. Exploring the history of Islam from its inception, engaging with the ideas and debates that scholars in the past have engaged with has allowed me to appreciate the sheer depth of the intellectual tradition which Islam manifests.” 

Nadim Pabani, Cohort 6
