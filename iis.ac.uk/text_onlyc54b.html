    Interview with Dr Shainool Jiwa on The Founder of Cairo

     

     

     

    This twenty-three min interview with Dr Shainool Jiwa provides an overview of the Fatimids, a Shi‘i Ismaili dynasty whose two and a half century rule extended from the shores of the Atlantic across the southern Mediterranean into Palestine, Syria and along the Red Sea coast, provides the first sustained experience of Shi‘i rule in the region. .. Focusing on the reign of the fourth Fatimid Imam-Caliph al-Mu‘izz li-Din Allah (953 - 975 CE), Dr Jiwa reflects on the principles of Fatimid governance and the creation of institutions that enabled the transformation of the Fatimid dynasty from a North African state into an expansive Mediterranean empire.

    Dr Jiwa’s study on the reign of Imam-Caliph al-Mui‘zz is based on the magisterial work of the 15th century Ismaili da‘i Idris ‘Imad al-Din’s (d.1468 CE) ‘Uyun al-akhbar wa funun al-athar, the fountainheads of history. As the chief da‘i of the Yemeni Tayyibi Ismailis, Idris composed the ‘Uyun as a record of the Ismaili Imamat from its inception upto his own time, drawing upon a rich repertoire of Ismaili and non-Ismaili sources that had been part of the Fatimid literary tradition.

    The‘Uyun is the only surviving medieval Ismaili work available to us that documents the history of the Fatimid dynasty. Dr Jiwa’s The Founder of Cairois the first annotated English translation of the chapter on al- Mu‘izz from the‘Uyun.

    1. Who were the Fatimids and what stimulated your interest in studying them in detail?

    2. You have made some pertinent observations about the modes of the Governance of the Fatimids. Could you please elaborate on these for our audiences?

    3. Which historical accounts on the Fatimids did you study for your work and how would you compare them?

    4. Could you please elaborate more on Idris Imam al-Din’s account?

    5. In what ways could the study of the Fatimids be of benefit to us today?
