    Music & Melodies of the Persian Ismaili Qasideh


    Introduction | Modes | Melodies | Musical Instruments | Qasideh Tradition | Qasideh Collection


    Melodies

    The five awazes, or melodies, are: Abu 'Ata, Bayat-i Isfahan, Bayat-i-Turk, Afshari and Dashti.

    Melody
    Abu 'AtaAbu 'Ata is related to Shur. However, Abu 'Ata does not sound like Shur, for it has a more serene character. It is often used in religious chanting.

    Bayat-i IsfahanPersian musicians commonly consider Bayat-i Isfahan to be a derivative of Homayun, but it is played as an independent mode. Bayat-i Isfahan is also considered to be the Persian counterpart of the harmonic minor mode of Western music.

    Bayat-i-TurkThis awaz is also occasionally known as Bayat-i Zand. Its lively nature puts it closer to Mahur but it could also be interpreted in the shades of Shur. Dervish chants are often in this mode.

    AfshariThis melody has a tragic and sombre character, similar to Shur. However, this characteristic changes later in the melody. One of the most famous awazes which is recited in Afshari is Mathnawi, which is often used in forms of Sufi poetic recitations.

    DashtiDashti, like Abu 'Ata, belongs to the family of Shur. However, Dashti relies on melodic ideas distinct from Shur. Dashti is very popular in folklore music of many areas of contemporary Iran.
