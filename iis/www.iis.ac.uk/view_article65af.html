    The Summer Programme on Islam

    Daily Schedule
     

    Monday 11 August

    Morning
     
    Diversity and Pluralism - Professor Zulfikar Hirji

    Objective(s):

    (a) Explore the importance of understanding diversity and pluralism when studying Islam;

    (b) Examine pluralism as a value and its importance in contemporary society; and

    (c) Provide frameworks for ways to think about diversity and pluralism in the contemporary context – challenges, implications, and opportunities.

     
    Afternoon
     
    Encountering Islam - Professor Zulfikar Hirji

    Objective(s):

    (a) Contextualise religious identity as part of the broader development of thought and culture in Muslim societies; and

    (b) Provide a framework for understanding the diversity of Muslim societies

     
    Evening
     
    Free Evening
     
     

    Tuesday 12 August

    Morning
     
    Authority: Depicting the Prophet - Dr Farouk Mitha

    Objective(s):

    (a) Provide participants with an introduction to the life of the Prophet;

    (b) Explore the historical Muhammad and the Ahmad of faith;

    (c) Explore the veneration of the Prophet; and (d) Examine images/ depictions of the Prophet and their contexts.

     
    Authority: Post-Prophetic Models of Authority – the Caliphate - Dr Farouk Mitha

    Objective(s):

    (a) Survey the transition from Prophetic to post-Prophetic authority and the formation of institutions of authority in religious traditions in general and Islam in particular;

    (b) Explore the institutions of leadership: caliphate and imamat; and

    (c) Explore the movement to preserve the memory of the Prophet through the formation of the institution of the sunnah and how this crystallises into the institution of the ulama as custodians of Prophetic tradition.

     
    Afternoon/Evening
     
    Cairo: Exploring a Civilisation – Past and Present – Part 1 - Professor Zulfiqar Hirji

    Objective(s):

    (a) Explore the evolution of the city, its civilisation, key figures and major junctures in history; and

    (b) Examine its varied architecture over the centuries including urban development and civic space.

     
    Topics in the Contemporary Muslim World: Law in Muslim Contexts - Dr Mohamed Keshavjee

    Objective(s):

    (a) Explore the development and institutionalisation of fiqh/shari`a in Sunni and Shi`i contexts;

    (b) Examine the adaptation of fiqh/shari`a to changing contexts; and

    (c) Examine contemporary issues around the understanding and interpretation of shari`a;

     
     
    Wednesday 13 August
     
    Morning

    Authority: Post-Prophetic Models of Authority – Shi`i Interpretations of Islam, Part 1 - Dr Farouk Mitha

    Objective(s):

    (a) Examine the development of the concept of Imamat amongst different Muslim communities over different times and places; and

    (b) Explore contemporary manifestations of Shi`i models of authority

     
    Authority: Post-Prophetic Models of Authority – Shi`i Interpretations of Islam, Part 2 - Dr Farouk Mitha

    Objective(s):

    (a) Highlight specifically the Ismaili interpretation of the Shi`i Imamat; and

    (b) Discuss the contemporary significance of Imamat both for an individual and in the life of a community/ society.

     
    Afternoon/Evening
     
    Cairo: Exploring a Civilisation – past and present – Part 2- Professor Zulfiqar Hirji

    Objective(s):

    (a) Through case studies, examine the social history of Cairo; and

    (b) Explore Cairo’s cultural elements including music, literature, arts, diversity etc.

     
    Topics in the Contemporary Muslim World: Muslim Values and Ethics - Dr Amyn B. Sajoo

    Objective(s):

    (a) Explore the foundations of ethics — religious and secular — and the relationship with law;

    (b) Inquire into sources of core Muslim values through Qur’anic verses, and teachings of the Prophet and the Shi`i Imams;

    (c) Compare Muslim ethics of tolerance, respect for human life, and search for knowledge with values promoted in contemporary societies; and

    (d) Discuss “moral reasoning” and ways in which this is applied in daily life, including biomedical issues.

     
     
    Thursday 14 August
     
    Morning
     
    Contemporary Expressions of Shi‘i Islam - Professor Karen Ruffle

    Objective(s):

    (a) Review of contemporary Shi`i communities – their commonalities, differences, geography and demography;

    (b) Examine the diversity of Shi`i rituals and devotional literature in the Muslim world; and

    (c) Discuss Shi`i iconography found in art, architecture, culture etc.

     
    Afternoon/Evening
     
    Cairo: Exploring a Civilisation – past and present – Part 3 - Professor Zulfiqar Hirji

    Objective(s):

    (a) Examine recent events in Cairo such as the Arab Spring;

    (b) Explore the city as a nucleus for connections - past, present and future; and

    (c) Discuss key challenges of a city such as Cairo.

     
    Topics in the Contemporary Muslim World: From the Historical to the Modern - Dr Alnoor Dhanani

    Objective(s):

    (a) Survey the Muslim world from Napoleon’s invasion of Egypt to Ataturk’s embrace of Western secularism;

    (b) Explore revival, renewal and reform movements from the 18th until the 20th century;

    (c) Explore implications of European colonisation, anti-colonial nationalism, ‘modern’ forms of national elites and the formation of new nation states;

    (d) Examine positions of liberal Islam "Modern" Islam and Islamism on issues of politics, governance, and the public role of Islam through a survey of the perspective of their key proponents (e.g. Qutb, Khomeini, an-Naim, Abd al-Raziq etc); and

    (e) Develop an appreciation for the range of relevant issues and challenges in contemporary Muslim societies (such as law and legislation, the role of non-Muslim minorities in majority Muslim states, the place for human rights in national discourse, the intersection of gender discourses on these and related issues)
     
     

    Friday 15 August
     
    Morning
     
    The Qur’an and its Interpretations: Conceptions of Revelations amongst the Religions of the Book - Professor Walid Saleh

    Objectives:

    (a) Discuss the concept of revelation and the variety of perspectives on this amongst Muslims; and

    (b) Explore the event, form and content of the Qur’an (its compilation, structure, organization, etc.) including historical and contemporary debates

     
    The Qur’an and its Interpretations: Experiencing the Qur’an - Professors Ruba Kana’an and Walid Saleh

    Objective(s):

    (a) Appreciate the ways in which Muslims experience the Qur’an (orally, aurally, artistically, etc.).

     
    Afternoon
     
    Cairo: Exploring a Civilisation – past and present – Part 4 - Professor Zulfiqar Hirji

    Field Trip to the Royal Ontario Museum

    Objectives:

    (a) Tour the Fatimid Cairo exhibit at the Royal Ontario Museum; and

    (b) Discuss how a city can preserve its heritage whilst continuing to advance as a cosmopolitan city.

     
    Evening
     
    Evening Activities (TBC)

     
     
    Saturday 16 August
     
    Morning
     
    The Qur’an and its Interpretations: Tafsir Tradition – past and present - Professor Walid Saleh

    Objectives:

    (a) Introduce the tafsir tradition: past and present;

    (b) Examine relevant verses of the Qur’an and their various interpretations; and

    (c) Discuss the similarities between Ismaili approaches to the Qur’an and tafasir of other Muslim traditions

     
    The Qur’an and its Interpretations: Contemporary Context - Professor Walid Saleh

    Objectives:

    (a) Through case studies, situate recent debates relevant to the Qur’an; and

    (b) Examine the diverse perspectives on the Qur’an in Muslim worldviews.

     
    Afternoon
     
    Off-site Field Excursion
     
    Evening
     
    Formal Dinner

     
     
    Sunday 17 August
     
    Morning
     
    Topics in the Contemporary Muslim World: Reviving the Caliphate and Imposing the Shari`a - The Resurgence of jihad in the 21st Century - Dr Alnoor Dhanani

    Objectives:

    (a) Analyse jihadi movements such as al-Qa’ida, ISIS/ISIL and Boko Haram including their origins and objectives;

    (b) Consider how state actors engage with jihadi movements;

    (c) Explore reasons for jihadi movements having a hegemonic hold on discourses amongst young Muslims and the media in particular contexts; and

    (d) Examine how historical diversity and plural voices of Muslim civilisations can contribute to contemporary Muslim discourses.

     
    Afternoon
     
    FAQ Session

    Group Presentations

    Participants begin to depart by 18.30pm.

    (Sessions, Objectives and Speakers are subject to change)
