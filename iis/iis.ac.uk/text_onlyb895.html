Music and Poetry from the Pamir Mountains Other Genres - Folksongs

Folksong 07
Folksong 28

The term ‘folksong’ is used for poems or songs which do not fit in the traditional genres and whose author is generally unknown. They are mostly composed in a simple style, but this is not an exclusive characteristic of the folksongs, as many quatrains and ghazals have the same style.

Other Genres - Hikayat

Hikayat 06

Versified stories are usually called hikayat, meaning ‘anecdote’ or ‘story.’ The hikayats form the lengthiest part of madoh, sometimes lasting for more than an hour. A hikayat in madoh almost always deals with a miracle attributed to a holy person, very often Imam ‘Ali.

Other Genres - Qasida

Qasida 01

Qasidas do not form a distinct genre within Tajik Badakhshani poetry. In view of their length and subject matter, qasidas occupy a position somewhere between ghazal and hikayat.

Other Genres - Munajat

Munajat 01

The munajat are a class of religious poetry in which supplications are addressed to God.

Other Genres - Mukhammas

Mukhammas 08

The stanzaic poem, or mukhammas, is one of the more popular verse forms in Tajik Badakhshan, occurring both in madoh and in other performance-genres. A recurrent theme of the mukhammas is praise of Imam ‘Ali and his companions but other themes also occur.

Other Genres - Quatrains

Quatrain 01

A relatively large part of the poetry in Badakhshan includes quatrains, made up of two lines or four half-lines. Quatrains can be found both in secular and religious performance-genres. The quatrain is often used as a means to link or introduce different poems in a performance. In madoh, a sole quatrain occurs as a sort of prayer in between different longer poems.
