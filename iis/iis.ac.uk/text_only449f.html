Istitar al-imam


Copied by Ishaq b. ‘Abd al-Qadir and written for his pupil Hasan ‘Ali b. Tayyib ‘Ali as mentioned in the colophon on the morning of Rajab 13, 1315/December 7, 1897.
82 pp (bound with Muhammad al-Yamani’s Sirat al-Hajib); 17 lines per page; 22.4 x 14/15 x 8cm; clear black naskhi; corrections and additions in the margin.
MS 1008 (ArI)

Composed at the time of al-‘Aziz, Kitab Istitar al-imam wa tafarruq al-du'at fi'l-jaza'ir li-talabi-hi (The Book of the Concealment of the Imam and the Departure of the da‘is to different “islands” in search of him) relates important details on ‘Abd Allah al-Akbar and his successors down to ‘Abd Allah al-Mahdi, when he founded the Fatimid dynasty. The narrative refers to the hardship suffered by ‘Abd Allah al-Akbar and the quest for him by the da‘is who had lost touch with him. The work also provides valuable information regarding the early da‘wa as well as the setbacks faced by ‘Abd Allah al-Mahdi in his departure from Salamiyya to the Mughreb.
