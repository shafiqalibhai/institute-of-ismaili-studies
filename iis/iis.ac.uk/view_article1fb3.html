Music and Poetry from the Pamir Mountains Hikayat 06

play sample

Place: Ishkashim
Date: 31st July, 1992
Performer(s): Dodikhudo Jum‘akhon, vocals and rubab; Bahodur Rahmatsho, daf

  1. nagahan ruze ba taqdir-i ilah
    karsaz-i sani‘-yi ‘arz-u sama
  2. meguzasht ‘isa kinar-i dajla’i
    did dar sahra fitada kalla’i
  3. kalla-yi pusida sar az tan juda
    gusht-u pust-i maghz-i u gashta fana
  4. kasa-yi chashmash pur az khak-i siyah
    har du gushash mur kanda marha
  5. yak su dandanra zi ham bigsikhta
    maghz-i u ba khak-u khun amikhta
  6. karda ‘isa su-yi an kalla nigah
    dar ta‘ajjub mand gufta ey ilah
  7. afaridi khalq ra az nur-i pak
    pas dalil afkanda’i ba ru-yi khak
  8. man chi danam ta ki in sargashta kist
    andar in ‘alam chi guna karda zist
  9. jabra’il amad zi nazdik-i ilah
    guft ya ‘isa chi mekhahi bikhah
  10. guft mekhaham az in pusida sar
    hal-i khud ba ma biguyad sar ba sar
  11. guft jabra’il ki bipurs az way sukhan
    ta biguyad hal-u ahwal-i kuhan
  12. kard ‘isa su-yi an kalla faraz
    guft ay kalla ba haqq-i be niyaz
  13. ey hal-i khud ba ma biguyi sar ba sar
    khubru budi tu khud ya zishtru
  14. jannati budi tu khud ya duzakhi
    mudkhuli budi tu ya mard-i sakhi
  15. kar-u kirdar-i tu dar ‘alam chi bud
    ahl-i ‘izzat bud ya mard-i juhud
  16. padishah budi tu khud ya mudkhuli
    muhtasham budi tu khud ya benawa
  17. an sar amad bahri-i ‘esa dar sukhan
    gufta ey ‘isa manam mard-i kuhan
  18. gush kun tu shamma’e az hal-i man
    ta shawi waqif tu az ahwal-i man
  19. padishah budam ba mulk-i misr-u-sham
    rafta bud az sharq-u gharbam jumla nam
  20. haft ta kishwar bud dar farman-i man
    ey mushtari metaft dar aywan-i man
  21. mal-i man az mal-i qarun besh bud
    hamchu qarun bar daram darwesh bud
  22. si hazaram turk-i atlaspush bud
    si hazaram halqa andar gush bud
  23. si hazaram banda-yi zangi ghulam
    si hazaram banda-yi rumi ba nam
  24. si hazaram bud kanizan chu mah
    an hama ba dawlat-u taj-u kulah
  25. si hazaram ushtur-i bur-i siyah
    an hama dar bar budand sal-u mah
  26. charsad sag bud waqt-i sayd-u taz
    dah hazaram charkh-u yuz-u baz
  27. mutbikhan dar kar mebudand mudam
    dah hazaram mutbikhe budand ghulam
  28. jumla miskinan-i darweshan-i shahr
    az ta‘am-i mutbikham burdand bahr
  29. nam-i man buda-st sultan jumjuma
    man shuban budam hama ‘alam rama
  30. nuh sad-u si sal shud az murdanam
    hasrat-i dunya ba ‘uqba burda am
  31. man chunin shahe budam man dar jahan
    hal-i man in ast ey ‘esa bidan
  32. guft ‘esa hal-i nazr-i jan bigu
    andar an dam dida-yi giryan bigu
  33. guft ruze man ba sayd-i ahuwan
    baz gashtam su-yi shahr-i khud rawan
  34. atlasam dar pa-yi asb andakhtand
    chatr-i shahi bar saram afrashtand
  35. chu rasidam bar sar-i ey in jay-i man
    larza’e bigrift sar ta pay-i man
  36. az tabiban-u hakiman-i jihan
    jumlagi hazir shudand dar yak zaman
  37. har yake meyamadand ba sad liwan
    chara mekardand nameshuda rahman
  38. gard-i margam dar rukh-i zardam nishast
    rah-i hasti dast-i alfazam bubast
  39. afta’e didam wale az nagahan
    surate payda shuda az asman
  40. surate didam wale ba char dast
    haft sar darad wale ba ru’i ast
  41. bud changalash chu changal-i palang
    chang bar janam fitada be darang
  42. mekashad az nakhuni ta pa miyan
    ya rasulu’llah chi guyam sharh-i an
  43. haybat-i u zad zabanamra girift
    chang bar zad rishta-yi janam girift
  44. sharbat-i margast bemaya chashid
    marg u ra u kuja bashad padid {naghz}
  45. ‘aysi-yi maryam biguyad jumjuma
    hal-i gurat ra bigu ba ma hama
  46. az ‘azab-i gur az bim-i suwal
    ta chi didi andar in daru’l-qarar
  47. guft marg ba markab-i chubin nishan
    ta bikardand suy-i guristan rawan
  48. ‘alamam dar nala-wu zari kunan
    pur zi jumla lashkar-i pir-u jawan

Gist: This is a story about Prophet Jesus who is said to be walking along the banks of the River Tigris when he comes across a skull lying on the ground. He wanted to know who the skull belonged to and so he implores God to make it speak. The skull tells its story: he was once a great king who ruled over a vast empire and had thousands of slaves and hundreds of hunting dogs and falcons. By telling this story, the poet reminds us that all men, whatever their social status on earth, are feeble in the face of death.
