    IIS Alumni Association bids welcome to the Class of 2007
    October 2007

    10th September 2007 saw the graduation of eight members of the Graduate Programme in Islamic Studies and Humanities (GPISH), Class of 2007. The graduation ceremony took place at the Ismaili Centre in London, and was attended by Prince Rahim, Prince Hussain and Princess Khaliya. Over 250 guests, including academics, supporters of the IIS, as well as staff, students and alumni of the IIS joined the ceremony to honour the graduates.

    The graduating Class of 2007 are Salman Alibhai, Marjan Esmaili, Hussein Hirji, Kamal Khan, Amaan Pardhan, Sherozkhon Qizilov, Azima Shukurbekova and Anisa Virji.

    In their final year, the graduates undertook diverse areas of study ranging from African studies, International Studies and Diplomacy to Curriculum, Pedagogy and Assessment. The Class of 2007 attended various eminent institutions including the Universities of Oxford, London (IOE, King’s College and SOAS) and Pennsylvania.

    These graduates are now part of the IIS Alumni body. On behalf of the IIS Alumni Association, we wish to congratulate them on their achievements and welcome them to the Association.
