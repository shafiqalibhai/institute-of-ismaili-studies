Doctoral Scholarship Programme

The Institute awards doctoral scholarships each year to suitable candidates who are interested in pursuing careers in Islamic studies. More specifically, the Doctoral Scholarships Programme aims (1) to encourage the study of certain neglected areas of Islamic and related studies, and (2) to foster a community of scholars in the areas of interest to the IIS.

Applications are expected to focus on any areas in which Islam is studied in one of its various manifestations (historical, theological, philosophical, legal, educational, political, ritual, cultural, etc.). Priority will be given to proposals on:

  • Ismaili Studies, understood as covering any field and discipline that deals with the Ismailis, their history or their thought;
  • Shi‘i Studies, including Shi‘i history, hadith, doctrines, law, philosophy or ritual;
  • Qur’anic Studies, including studies on the Qur’an, its reception and interpretations (tafsir, ta’wil)

Other topics of Islamic Studies will also be considered, especially Fiqh, Hadith, Philosophy.

Attention will be given to research proposals conceived from various perspectives and disciplines related to the Humanities (including Anthropology, Arts and Material Culture, Curriculum and Education, Literature, Musicology, Political Science, Sociology, etc.). Applications focusing on historical studies (until the 19th century) as well as those concentrating on contemporary studies (20th century onwards) will be considered. Research may be focused on any region of the Muslim world.

Candidates must have an excellent academic record. Applications must include a research proposal that demonstrates analytical ability, methodological prowess and conceptual sophistication as well as a good knowledge of existing literature. The proposal must be written clearly and cover the main issues with intellectual depth and thoroughness. (The same applies to other material submitted by the candidates, such as writing samples). Candidates will be expected to have linguistic competence in languages traditionally used by Muslims (ideally Arabic or Persian and at least one other language) and in European languages (English and ideally at least one other). Preference is given to those applicants who have completed the IIS’ GPISH and STEP programmes.

The scholarship covers university and college fees, normally for a period of 3 years, as well as a living stipend of £12,900 per annum. The scholarship will be granted on the basis of a 50 percent grant and a 50 percent loan. However, the annual renewal of the award is conditional upon the student’s satisfactory academic progress. By accepting the Scholarship, the successful candidate enters a commitment, after completion of study and if requested by the IIS, to contribute to the work of the IIS or other related institutions (AKDN or the Ismaili Community). When possible, the IIS will have first right of publication of the final dissertation produced by the successful candidate.

Applications for this year’s programme is now closed.

The next application deadline is 30 April 2015.

Application reviews will take place in May 2015. Shortlisted candidates may be called for an interview in June 2015. The successful candidate will receive notice by late June/early July 2015. No legal entitlement shall be constituted by applying to the programme. Reasons for rejection will not be disclosed.

Follow this link for further information: “How to apply”. For any other information, you may email us on scholarships@iis.ac.uk.
