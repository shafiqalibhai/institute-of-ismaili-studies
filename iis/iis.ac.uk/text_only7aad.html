    The Summer Programme on Islam

    Further Information
     

    Accommodation and Venue

    Accommodation is booked for all participants, faculty members and staff at:

    Executive Learning Centre at Schulich
    York University
    4700 Keele St.
    Toronto, On M3J 1P3
    Canada

    Website: http://www.acc-schulichexecutiveconferencecentre.com/

     
    Accommodation is provided for the nights of 10 – 16 August, inclusive. Participants are expected to depart Toronto starting from 18.30 on Sunday 17 August. If your flight schedule requires you to stay the night of 09 or 17 August in Toronto, please contact spi2014@iicanada.org and we may be able to assist you in finding suitable accommodation. Please note that you will be responsible for the cost of the extra night(s) of accommodation and any associated costs. Participants are also required to arrange their own accommodation for any extra nights prior to the commencement of the programme.

    Participants’ rooms are equipped with a shower and towels. There are basic tea/coffee-making facilities in room. Each participant must have checked out with the porter at the main entrance and returned their room keys on the day of departure from the Executive Learning Centre, Sunday 17 August, before 08:30.

     
    Insurance

    All participants must ensure that they have adequate health and travel insurance whilst attending the programme.

     
    Transportation

    Participants are responsible for their return airfare to Toronto Pearson International Airport. Travel arrangements to and from the airport to the Executive Learning Centre can be arranged through the SPI team when sending in flight confirmation details. Excursions during the programme will be covered by course fees.

     
    Meals

    Breakfast, lunch and dinner are provided at York University. If you are allergic to any particular food, please email spi2014@iicanada.org so that appropriate arrangements can be made for you.

     
    Dress code

    The dress code during the academic sessions is casual. The Formal Dinner on Saturday evening (16 August) is semi-formal/business or cultural attire.

    The weather in Toronto during August is pleasant. However, please bring a sweater and a jacket as it may be chilly in the evenings and in the air conditioned halls.

     
    Telephone

    Each room is equipped with a telephone. Any international and long distance calls will be billed to the participant when they check out. Participants can purchase Calling Cards if necessary.

     
    Internet Access

    Internet connection is available in the rooms. Login information will be provided at the time of check-in. Wireless facility is also available in the main lecture room.

     
    Canada’s Power Supply

    Canada's electrical supply is 110 Volts and 60 Hertz (cycles per second).For visitors arriving from countries that use a different electrical supply/ electrical outlet, here is a website about international electricity that provides an explanation with photos of the adapters and transformers you may require for your electrical devices.

     
    Getting to York University

    If you are making your own way to campus, please see details below.

    From Toronto Pearson International Airport

    Exit the Airport and take Hwy 409 north, proceed to Hwy 401 east. Travel east on Hwy 401 to Hwy 400 north. Take Hwy 400 north to Steeles Avenue. Exit onto Steeles Avenue east. Follow Steeles Avenue east to Keele Street. At Keele Street turn right going south, the campus is located on the right. Turn right onto the campus via The Pond Road entrance, the second stop light after Steeles. Take The Pond Road to James Gillies Street, the Student Services Parking Garage will be visible on the left. The Executive Learning Centre is next door to the Student Services Parking Garage, on Fine Arts Road, adjacent to the Schulich School of Business..

    Public Transportation

    Several buses are routed to the campus or have express services to York University from the Subway. Below are a few options:

    From Downsview Station on the Yonge University Line – Take 196 York U Rocket.

    From Finch Station on the Yonge Line – Take 60C or 60F.

    From Jane Station on the Bloor Line – Take 35E

     
    Important Documents

    All participants should ensure they carry the following:

    · Passport (with a photocopy) and other personal identification;

    · Medical insurance;

    · Health card;

    · Special medical condition advisory badges (if applicable);

    · Emergency contact information;

    · Limited personal cash and access to credit for emergency and personal needs.

     
    Disclaimer

    Please be advised that participants are solely responsible for themselves and the personal items that they bring to Canada. York University,Executive Learning Centre at Schulich, Ismaili Council for Canada, ITREB Canada, The Institute of Ismaili Studies and any other Institution involved in the organization of this programme will not be held responsible for any loss or damage to personal belongings or any health related issues.
