Tuesday 14th August

Morning

Participants arrive and make their way to Cambridge by 2.30pm: Arrival and check-in until 3.30pm

Afternoon

Opening Remarks by Mr Shiraz Kabani Logistics and Evaluation by Selina K Ramji

Evening

Networking & Mingling

Wednesday 15th August

Morning

Depicting the Prophet: Constructions and their contexts by Dr Alnoor Dhanani

Objective(s):

  1. Provide participants with an introduction to the life of the Prophet
  2. Explore the historical Muhammad and the Ahmad of faith
  3. Explore the veneration of the Prophet
  4. Examine images/depictions of the Prophet and their contexts

Afternoon

God’s Revelation: Houris in paradise or eternal hellfire? By Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal

Objective(s):

  1. Explore the event, form and content of the Quran.
  2. Explore debates (both historical and contemporary) on the origin, collection and canonisation of the Quran.
  3. Examine perspectives on the Quran in Muslim worldviews

Evening

Punting on the Cam

Thursday 16th August

Morning

After the Prophet: Authority, Legitimacy and Institutionalisation I by Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal and Dr Alnoor Dhanani

Objective(s):

  1. Survey the transition from Prophetic to post-Prophetic authority and the formation of institutions of authority in religious traditions in general and in the particular case of Islam
  2. Explore the institutions of leadership: caliphate; imamate; the emergence of the Shia understanding of post-Prophetic leadership and authority
  3. Explore the movement to preserve the memory of the Prophet through the formation of the institution of the sunna and how this crystallises into the institution of the ulama as custodians of Prophetic tradition

Afternoon

After the Prophet: Authority, Legitimacy and Institutionalisation II by Dr Alnoor Dhanani and Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal

Objective(s):

  1. Explore the development and institutionalisation of fiqh/sharia in the Sunni and Shia contexts
  2. Examine the adaptation of fiqh/sharia to changing contexts
  3. Explore charismatic authority of Sufi pirs/shaykhs as a form of authority and the reasons for the development of the institution of Sufi tariqas

Evening

Diversity & Pluralism
Through film the session will explore the pluralism that exists amongst Muslims.

Friday 17th August

Morning

From the historical to the modern Dr Alnoor Dhanani

Objective(s):

  1. Survey the Muslim world from Napoleon’s invasion of Egypt to Ataturk’s embrace of Western secularism
  2. Explore Revival, Renewal and Reform movements from 18th until early 20th century
  3. Explore implications of European colonisation, anti-colonial nationalism, “modern” forms of national elites and the formation of new nation states
  4. Explore the shrinkage of classical fiqh/sharia to the domain of only “family law” and the adoption of Western legal codes

Afternoon

Modern Islam, Islamism and Gender Perspectives by Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal

Objective(s):

  1. Situate the rise of Islamism (e.g. the Iranian Revoluton, jihad in Afghanistan, Talibans, Salafis, Wahhabis): the role of jihad, sharia and the Islamic State, women and non-Muslim minorities
  2. Explore the opposition to Islamist movements (such as spiritual brotherhoods, secularists)
  3. Explore the impact of globalisation

Evening

General Question and Answer session to provide a forum in which participants can raise any issues from the week (or even those not explored during the week) which require further elucidation.

Saturday 18th August

Morning

A clash of civilisations? Islamophobias and Westphobias by Dr Alnoor Dhanani and Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal

Objective(s):

  1. Explore contemporary constructions of ‘Islam’ and the ‘West’ as mutual ‘others’
  2. Explore the global reach of Islamism rooted in Arabia (role of petro-dollars, standardised Quran, notions of equating Islam with Arabs, etc.)
  3. Explore the impact of Western economic and cultural globalisation on Muslim societies
  4. Explore the causes and possible outcomes of the Arab Spring

Afternoon

Reclaiming Islamic heritage and building for the future by Mr Khalil Shariff

Objective(s):

  1. Explore current global Imamat initiatives and their vision and underlying principles
  2. Discuss the impact of the above for the Jamat, especially with regard to addressing issues faced by Muslims
  3. Explore both short and long term impact of this work and how it can be used as an entry point for thoughtful engagement

Evening

Formal Dinner

Biographies

Faculty

Dr Alnoor Dhanani

Dr Alnoor Dhanani specialises in the intellectual history of Islamic civilisation, in particular the interactions between science, theology, and philosophy in historical and contemporary contexts. He has taught courses related to the study of Islam at various institutions including Harvard, Tuftsand Brown University and at The Institute of Ismaili Studies in London, where he was the Head of the Department of Graduate Studies. He is currently a research fellow at the Department of Near Eastern Languages and Cultures of Harvard University and is working on the history of atomism after Avicenna in the Islamic milieu. His other research projects are in the area of historical and contemporary natural philosophy and cosmology including how Muslim atomists explained personal identity following resurrection and contemporary theological speculation on the Big Bang theory of the origin of the universe.

Dr Aziz Esmail

Dr Aziz Esmail is the former Dean of The Institute of Ismaili Studies (1988-98) and, since 1998, has served as one of its Governors. Originally trained in philosophy, literature and religion, Dr Esmail has held lecturing and research positions at various American universities as well as the University of Nairobi, Kenya. He has been associated with The Committee on Social Thought at the University of Chicago as well as Harvard University’s Center for the Study of World Religions and Graduate School of Education. Dr Esmail has been a consultant to, among other institutions, the Humanities division of the Rockefeller Foundation. In addition to articles in journals, his writings include The Poetics of Religious Experience (I. B. Tauris in association with The Institute of Ismaili Studies, 1996) and A Scent of Sandalwood (I. B. Tauris in association with The Institute of Ismaili Studies, 2002).


In addition to his responsibilities as a Governor, Dr Esmail contributes to the Institute in an executive capacity, chiefly in the formulation of the intellectual strategies which inform the work of its various departments.

Dr Nadia Eboo Jamal

Dr Nadia Eboo-Jamal received her Ph.D. in Near Eastern Languages and Literature from New York University in 1996 and is a specialist in Persian history and culture in the period of Mongol rule, with a particular emphasis on the Ismaili communities of the time.

Over the past fifteen years, Dr Eboo-Jamal has been actively involved in varying capacities with the Ismaili Tariqah and Religious Education Boards in the United Kingdom and the United States, providing her expertise and assistance to teaching activities, developing educational materials and human resources and contributing to the Ismaili community’s diverse religious education programmes. She is currently a Senior Lecturer in the Graduate Studies Department and a member of staff in the Curriculum Studies Department at the Institute of Ismaili Studies.

She is the author of Surviving the Mongols: Nizari Quhistani and the Continuity of Ismaili Tradition in Iran (London: I.B. Tauris in association with The Institute of Ismaili Studies, 2002) and is currently engaged in several projects, including a translation and critical edition of Nizari Quhistani’s “Safarnama” and a critical edition and translation of a manuscript entitled, “Kitab Ta’wil al-Shari’a” a work on Fatimid Ismaili thought as presented by Imam al-Mui‘zz li-Din Allah and Qadi al-Nu‘man.

Mr Khalil Shariff

Mr Khalil Shariff joined Aga Khan Foundation Canada as Chief Executive Officer in August 2005. He was previously with the Toronto office of McKinsey & Company, an international management consultancy, where he advised governments, financial institutions, and health care providers on strategy, organisation, and operational improvement.

Khalil served on AKFC’s National Committee for five years, and has cultivated his interest in international development and conflict resolution issues through a variety of activities including as: Deputy Editor-in-Chief, Harvard Negotiation Law Review; Policy Co-ordinator and Research Associate, Harvard Program on Humanitarian Policy and Conflict Research; Legal Intern, Chambers of the Vice-President, International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda in Arusha, Tanzania; Intern, Office of Under-Secretary-General, UN Department of Peacekeeping Operations. He was the youngest member ever elected as a School Trustee in 1993 for the Board of School Trustees in Richmond, B.C.

Khalil holds a B.A. in International Relations and Economics from the University of British Columbia and a J.D. magna cum laude from the Harvard Law School.

IIS Staff

Nuruddin Badruddin

Nuruddin Badruddin joined the Department of Community Relations (DCR) as the Programmes Coordinator and ITREB Liaison Officer in May 2007. He is responsible for the coordination and management of international programmes organised by the department as well as developing structured relationships between the IIS and the relevant Jamati and Imamat institutions.

Before joining the IIS, Nuruddin was the Executive Officer at the Ismaili Tariqah and Religious Education Board in the United Arab Emirates since 2003. Prior to that, Nuruddin worked in senior management roles in information systems in Montreal, Miami and Karachi.

Born in Khulna, then East Pakistan, and raised in Karachi, he graduated from the University of Karachi with a Bachelors degree in Commerce. He has long-standing experience of serving Imamat and Jamati institutions in Pakistan, USA, Canada and the UAE.

Fatima Dhanani

Fatima Dhanani gained a BSc in Anatomy and Cell Biology with Management from McGill University in 2004.She then went on to study for a LLB Bachelors of Law at the University of Leicester, which she completed in 2007.In 2008, she completed her Legal Practice Course (LPC) at BPP Law School London, before returning briefly to Canada.

She joined the Department of Community Relations at the IIS as Programmes Officer in May 2009. Subsequently, she took on the role as the Education Officer. In June 2011, Miss Dhanani transitioned to the position of Research Officer for the Constituency Studies Unit in the Co-Directors’ Office. In this role she supports the coordination of the research agenda as well as the development of relevant proposals and strategies.

Fatima is currently pursuing her Masters in Islamic Law at the School of Oriental and African Studies (SOAS), University of London.

Hussain Jasani

Hussain Jasani is Manager for Educational Programmes in the Department of Community Relations. In this role, he provides oversight and direction to the International Training Programmes (IWTP, PTEP and IAEP) as well as other educational programmes including training for the Ismaili Tariqah and Religious Education Boards as well as orientation and refresher programmes for other constituencies.

Originally from India, Hussain obtained his first degree in Electronics in 1988. He completed a Diploma in Religious Education (1991) from the Institute of Education, University of London, before joining the IIS’ Waezeen and Teacher Education Programme in 1992. Subsequently, he obtained an MA in Islamic Societies and Cultures (1995) from the School of Oriental and African Studies, University of London.

Prior to joining the IIS in July 2012, Hussain was the Chief Executive Officer of ITREB for India for over a decade. During his 17 year tenure with ITREB India, he also worked as the Head of Human Resource Development, Research and Development as well as in its Education wing.

Hussain has presented academic papers at seminars held at The Sorbonne University, The University of Saurashtra, Bombay University, and conducted HR Development workshops for many government, corporate and non-governmental organisations. His contribution in the academic and social sector has resulted in his name being included in multiple editions of Asia Pacific Who’s Who by Rifacimento International since 2000.

Shiraz Kabani

Shiraz Kabani joined The Institute of Ismaili Studies in September 1999. After serving as Executive Officer (1999-2000), Head of Resource Development and Communications (2000-05), and Head of Operations, Finance & Development (2005-11), he now heads the Department of Community Relations.

This department includes the portfolios of Community Education Programmes, International Training Programmes, STEP Deployment in the Field, Alumni Relations, Project Tajikistan, Country Strategies to Enhance ITREBs, and HRD Programme for Central Asia. He also serves as acting head of the Department of Communications and Development, overseeing resource development, communications, and the IIS website. In addition, Mr Kabani is Secretary to the IIS Board of Governors, which is chaired by His Highness the Aga Khan.

In addition to a BSc in Electrical Engineering & Computer Science and an MBA, Mr Kabani is currently pursuing a doctoral degree in Management, focussing on ethics and organisational leadership. His professional experience includes eleven years as Executive Officer of the Ismaili Tariqah and Religious Education Board for the USA and as the Managing Director of an IT consulting firm in Houston.

Over the past 25 years, Mr Kabani has served as a public speaker and faculty member for human resource development programmes of the IIS and Ismaili community institutions in North America and Europe. He has also participated in the conceptualisation of various international projects for the Ismaili community. Currently, he is the Vice President of the Ismaili Council for the United Kingdom.

Nina Lee

She is responsible for the design and implementation of the intellectual content of educational programmes undertaken by the department and the continued professional development of Jamati educators.
